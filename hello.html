Description:

  • The Upanishads are ancient Hindu philosophical texts that explore the nature of existence, the self (Atman), and the ultimate reality (Brahman).
  • They mark the transition from ritualistic traditions of the Vedas to a more introspective and spiritual inquiry, forming the foundation of Vedanta philosophy.
  • The Upanishads teach that Brahman is the infinite, unchanging essence of the universe, and Atman (the individual soul) is identical to it.
  • Key concepts include Moksha (liberation), Maya (illusion), and Karma (action and consequence).
  • Through dialogues between sages and disciples, they emphasize self-inquiry, meditation, and knowledge (Jnana Yoga) as the path to enlightenment.
  • Their timeless wisdom has influenced Hinduism, Buddhism, and global philosophical traditions, making them a cornerstone of spiritual thought.
