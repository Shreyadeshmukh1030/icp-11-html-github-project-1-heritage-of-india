History of Indian Heritage

India's rich cultural heritage spans thousands of years, with a diverse array of influences and traditions.

Ancient India (3300 BCE - 500 CE)

The Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's oldest urban civilizations, thrived in the northwestern part of the subcontinent.

Medieval India (500 - 1500 CE)

The Gupta Empire, known as the "Golden Age of India," saw significant advances in science, technology, and the arts.

Mughal Era (1526 - 1756 CE)

The Mughal Empire, founded by Babur, brought a unique blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic influences to the region.

British Colonial Era (1757 - 1947 CE)

The British East India Company's arrival marked the beginning of colonial rule, which had a profound impact on Indian culture and society.

Modern India (1947 CE - present)

India gained independence in 1947, and has since become a vibrant democracy, with a thriving cultural scene and a strong sense of national identity.
